Research Experience

  • Doctoral Thesis Student, KAIST, South Korea (2020 – Present)
    Advisor: Prof. Shuping Xiong
    • Conducted a systematic review to identify key obstacles in existing modeling methods for exoskeletons.
    • Developed a novel kinematic model for simulating shoulder range of motion (ROM), demonstrating high accuracy (0.3%-14.3% discrepancy) between simulated and measured data.
    • Built a simplified kinetic model for predicting static balance in exoskeleton users, achieving strong correlations with subjective instability ratings (r=0.79) and small differences from existing models.
    • Developing an experiment-free musculoskeletal modelling solution for shoulder exoskeletons.
    • Constructing a general modelling framework for the ergonomic evaluation of industrial exoskeletons, designed to streamline model development for researchers, saving time and effort in design and evaluation processes.
  • Master Thesis Student, Jilin University, China (2017 – 2020)
    Advisor: Prof. Jindong Ren
    • Examined the relationship between human body dimension, seat setting, seat pressure distribution, and user comfort.
    • Developed a pressure distribution-based comfort evaluation method, showing a strong correlation between movement frequency and comfort level.
    • Created a data-driven comfort seat position distribution model considering user anthropometry and seat angles.
